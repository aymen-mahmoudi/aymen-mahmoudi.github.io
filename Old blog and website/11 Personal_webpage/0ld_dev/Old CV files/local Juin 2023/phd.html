PhD journey


Certainly, the PhD is one of the best journey a person can experience. I am very glad to have this opportunity and be involved in such experience.
I have started my Ph.D. trip at the Centre for Nanosciences and Nanotechnologies C2N in Palaiseau. CNRS-FRANCE. I am well received by the MAT2D team members who are interested in different kinds of bidimensional materials.
My thesis page on Thése.fr website : https://www.theses.fr/s308409

My PhD is a part of the theme of two-dimensional (2D) materials of atomic thickness which are nowadays considered with attention in view of future applications and more fundamental studies. In particular, this work is devoted to monolayers of transition metal dichalcogenides (TMD) MX2 (M = Mo, W ; X =S, Se, Te). Due to their intrinsic band gap of about 1.1 to 1.9 eV, these materials are considered good candidates for field effect transistor (FET) devices.
By implementing microscopy and spectroscopy techniques such as Raman, photoemission and absorption spectroscopy, a study of the electronic, optical and structural properties of heterostructures composed of several 2D materials has been performed in order to provide a better understanding of these emerging systems.

Characterization facilities I used


In this section, I will list the most important skills I earned through my Ph.D. as well as the experiments I have done.

Optical spectroscopy

For optically characterizing the 2D flakes I use Raman and Photoluminsence very often.

AFM

Another technique I am often using it is AFM, In our clean room, we use Brucker and ParkSystem machines.

TEM

Thanks to the help of Fabrice Oehler, I got TEM data on the heterostructures I have. With him, we have transfered CVD grown flakes on carbon covered copper grid and performed TEM measurement on WSe2.

2D transfer techniques

2D transfer techniques are essential to master. During my Ph.D. I used both wet and dry methods to build to make 2D heterostructures

ARPES

ARPES experimeent were a such part of my thesis, All the expirement I made were in the different beamline of Synchrotron Soleil in France (Cassioppée, Antares and Tempo). For the analysis part, I used IgorPro and Python based softwares (check the Computer skills tab). Using ARPES and XPS, I have investigated the band structure of the different vdW heterostructures I am dealing with.
